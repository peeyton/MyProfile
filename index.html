Project Idea

My project idea is pressure-sensor pad (“pad”) for any replenishable item, whether that be in the fridge, the pantry or cleaning cupboard. The idea is to create pads that connect to a mobile app on your phone which allows the user to access the mobile app whilst out of the house and see which items they may need to purchase.

I can never remember what I have in the house in the way of milk, bread, toilet paper and other replenishable items. Every time I go to the supermarket on my way home from work, or friends’ places or elsewhere, I find myself calling my dad or brother find out if we need anything. If I can’t get one of them on the phone, I find that I regularly forget something and need to go back to the supermarket either that night if it is required for dinner preparation or I go the next day if it’s not so urgent. Given the current COVID-19 situation, going to the supermarket regularly is not ideal. Also given the current COVID-19 situation, easily finding statistics on how often people are attending the supermarket and forgetting items is difficult as supermarket related statistics are in relation to COVID-19 and the hysteria when supermarkets were being all but raided. I believe this is one of those problems where if it’s relevant to me, it could be relevant to many.

My idea for the pads would be to use the pads along with a linked mobile app. The pads will be able to sense the weight of the item in which is placed on it. The pad will be able to transmit this information to the mobile app.

The pads would need to be small and compact but accurate when measuring the weight of the item placed on it. When conducting some research on how this would be possible, I came across an article from 2016 where a prototype was created in relation to this exact idea. Information in relation to the prototype can be found here: “Building a Smart Fridge Weight Scale” by Paula Petcu. The prototype that was made is a pad with bulky wires and a separate battery plugged in.

Ideally the mobile app will have association with the online catalogues of supermarkets. The four biggest supermarkets, being Coles, Woolworths, ALDI and IGA all have online catalogues and online ordering services. These four supermarkets make up 84.3% of the industry. If the app was to be linked with all four catalogues, I believe this would make the pads more marketable to the majority of the country.

The mobile app will require user input in the form of identifying the item which is placed on it with as much accuracy as possible. By having the catalogues linked to the app, the user will be able to select the items in which they usually purchase to give the most accuracy of the items weight. The mobile app will also include a shopping list. This will include a list of all items which the user has input and a subsequent automated shopping list that users can access from anywhere.

There are already scales which are linked with a mobile app. These scales are predominately used for fitness. They tell the user not only their weight but also other information in relation to body fat percentage, muscle mass, BMI and many other things. These scales and the associated mobile apps could be adapted to the use being proposing here.

The biggest obstacle I envisage is not actually in relation to the pads or the mobile app but in relation to the batteries used. Batteries do not perform very well in the cold and therefore there will be difficulties found when keeping the pads in the fridge.

Another obstacle which could hinder this product is that the user may find that keeping items in the exact same spot to be tedious and the pads will need for the items to be placed precisely on them and centered to produce the most accurate results.

In relation to the hardware needed to complete this project, I will refer you to “How to Build Arduino Weighing Scales” by p_leriche. In this article there is a range of products that they have used to build a digital scale. In terms of software, this will need a lot more research as each mobile app is different and the software needed for this project is currently out of my range of knowledge. Kogan have a smart weighing scale which connects to their mobile app Kogan SmarterHome app. Investigating how this scale and mobile app work together would be an ideal starting point.

In order to create this project, an Application Developer would be needed to write the mobile app. They may need extensive experience to incorporate all parts of the mobile app together however I believe this should be fairly straight forward for someone with the knowledge to do so. To construct the pads, someone who is an electrical and mechanical technician would be able to put these together. The hardest part will be making the two talk to each other, however with some investigation into how smart watches and phones connect we would be able to find this out.

If this project was to succeed, it would mean less trips to the supermarket as you will always be able to grab the items which you need the first time you go. For the amount of effort and time required to create this project it may not be feasible in the long run to combat the simple issue at hand. The mobile app will need to be marketed at a very low cost or for free as users may not see the sense in paying for such a mobile app. Supermarkets may not be supportive of such a project as the more times someone attends the supermarket, the more opportunity the supermarket has for advertising and for impulse purchases by customers.
